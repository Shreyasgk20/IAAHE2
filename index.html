Institutional Characteristics:

• Type of Institution:

Classification by Type of Institution

Public Higher Education Institutions (HEIs)
HEIs that that are funded mainly by state, provincial, prefectural, or national governments
Private Not-for-Profit Higher Education Institutions (HEIs)
HEIs that operate as private nonprofit educational organizations and rely more heavily on student tuition fees, alumni donations, and endowments to fund their academic programs
Private For-Profit Higher Education Institutions (HEIs)
HEIs that operate as private for-profit businesses and rely principally on student tuition fees in order to sustain operations

• Enrollment Size:

Classification by Enrollment Size

Small Institutions
Institutions with a full-time equivalent (FTE) enrollment of fewer than 3000 degree-seeking / diploma-seeking students
Medium-Size Institutions
Institutions with a full-time equivalent (FTE) enrollment of 3000-9999 degree-seeking / diploma-seeking students
Large Institutions
Institutions with a full-time equivalent (FTE) enrollment of 10,000 or more degree-seeking / diploma-seeking students

// Classification by Type of Institution

• Educational Orientation:

Classification by Educational Orientation

Teaching-Oriented Institutions
Institutions for which: • the number of teaching faculty* comprise more than 50% of the total number of faculty members – or – • their statements of mission, vision, goals, objectives, and / or identity emphasize teaching excellence, student-centeredness, student success, and / or undergraduate education
Research-Oriented Institutions
Institutions for which: • the number of research faculty* comprise 50% or more of the total number of faculty members – or – • their statements of mission, vision, goals, objectives, and / or identity emphasize research, scholarly publication, knowledge creation, thought leadership, and / or graduate education

Faculty Types *

• Teaching Faculty: Faculty members for whom teaching is more heavily weighted than research, scholarly publication, and other professional expectations in (a) faculty evaluations, (b) tenure or promotion decisions, and / or (c) contractual work duties and responsibilities
• Research Faculty: Faculty members for whom research and scholarly publication are more heavily weighted than teaching and other professional expectations in (a) faculty evaluations, (b) tenure or promotion decisions, and / or (c) contractual work duties and responsibilities

// End of Classification by Educational Orientation

• Program Range:

Classification by Program Range

Limited Scope Institutions
Institutions that: • offer at least 50% of their degrees / diplomas in just one program disciplinary area (PDA)* – or – • offer degrees / diplomas in no more than 5 PDA* fields
Broad Scope Institutions
Institutions: • for which the degrees / diplomas offered in any one program disciplinary area* comprise less than 50% of the total number of degrees / diplomas offered – and – • that offer degrees / diplomas in more than 5 PDA* fields

• Degree / Diploma Level:

Classification by Degree / Diploma Level

Institutions with Exclusively Short-Cycle Programs at the Undergraduate Level
Institutions that offer undergraduate degrees / diplomas in programs exclusively at ISCED-Level 5 *
Institutions with Predominantly Short-Cycle Programs at the Undergraduate Level
Institutions that: • offer undergraduate degrees / diplomas in programs at both ISCED-Level 5 and ISCED-Level 6 * – and – • offer 51-99% of their undergraduate degrees / diplomas in programs at ISCED-Level 5 *
Institutions with Exclusively Baccalaureate Programs at the Undergraduate Level
Institutions that offer undergraduate degrees / diplomas in programs exclusively at ISCED-Level 6 *
Institutions with Predominantly Baccalaureate Programs at the Undergraduate Level
Institutions that: • offer undergraduate degrees / diplomas in programs at both ISCED-Level 5 and ISCED-Level 6 * – and – • offer 50-99% of their undergraduate degrees / diplomas in programs at ISCED-Level 6 *
Institutions with Exclusively Master’s Programs at the Graduate Level
Institutions that offer graduate degrees / diplomas in programs exclusively at ISCED-Level 7 *
Institutions with Predominantly Master’s Programs at the Graduate Level
Institutions that: • offer graduate degrees / diplomas in programs at both ISCED-Level 7 and ISCED-Level 8 * – and – • offer 51-99% of their graduate degrees / diplomas in programs at ISCED-Level 7 *
Institutions with Exclusively Doctoral Programs at the Graduate Level
Institutions that offer graduate degrees / diplomas in programs exclusively at ISCED-Level 8 *
Institutions with Predominantly Doctoral Programs at the Graduate Level
Institutions that: • offer graduate degrees / diplomas in programs at both ISCED-Level 7 and ISCED-Level 8 * – and – • offer 50-99% of their graduate degrees / diplomas in programs at ISCED-Level 8 *

ISCED Categories *

• ISCED-Level 5: Short-Cycle Tertiary Education Programs (usually 1-year or 2-year undergraduate programs)
• ISCED-Level 6: Bachelor’s or Equivalent Programs
• ISCED-Level 7: Master’s or Equivalent Programs
• ISCED-Level 8: Doctoral or Equivalent Programs

* The International Standard Classification of Education (ISCED) is a comprehensive framework developed by UNESCO for categorizing education programs and qualifications according to a uniform and internationally-accepted set of criteria. Its purpose is to facilitate cross-national comparisons of education systems and statistics.

Supplemental data sheet

Examples

• Learning Management Systems (LMS): centralized platforms designed to facilitate the planning, delivery, and evaluation of educational content; LMS are used to (a) streamline teaching and learning processes across physical or virtual classrooms and (b) enhance learner engagement, progress tracking, and content accessibility; they require instructors and learners to interact digitally through assignments, discussions, and assessments, thereby improving digital literacy, time management, and self-paced learning.
• Artificial Intelligence (AI): the simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems, for the purpose of (a) enabling systems to perform tasks such as learning, reasoning, problem-solving, and decision-making, and (b) improving efficiency, accuracy, and automation across various domains; AI applications require users to interact with intelligent algorithms and data-driven models, fostering analytical thinking, innovation, and adaptability to emerging technologies.
• Interactive Whiteboards (IWBs): touch-sensitive digital display boards that allow users to interact with content using a pen, finger, or stylus for the purpose of (a) enhancing classroom engagement through dynamic visual presentations and (b) facilitating collaborative learning and real-time feedback; IWBs require students and teachers to engage with multimedia resources, encouraging active participation, creativity, and improved understanding of complex concepts.
• Digital Textbooks: electronic versions of traditional textbooks accessible on digital devices for the purpose of (a) providing interactive, multimedia-rich learning experiences and (b) ensuring easy access to up-to-date educational content anytime, anywhere; digital textbooks require learners to navigate hyperlinks, videos, and interactive assessments, promoting self-directed learning, technological proficiency, and deeper subject comprehension.
• Online Assessment Platforms: web-based tools used to create, deliver, and evaluate tests and quizzes for the purpose of (a) measuring student learning outcomes efficiently and (b) providing instant feedback and analytics to improve teaching strategies; these platforms require learners to complete assessments in a digital environment, enhancing time management, digital skills, and academic accountability.
• Virtual Classrooms: online learning environments that enable real-time interaction between teachers and students through video conferencing, chat, and shared digital resources for the purpose of (a) replicating traditional classroom experiences remotely and (b) promoting flexible, location-independent education; virtual classrooms require active participation through digital tools, fostering communication skills, self-discipline, and adaptability to remote learning.
• Personalized Learning Platforms: adaptive educational systems that tailor content, pace, and learning paths to individual student needs for the purpose of (a) maximizing student engagement and understanding and (b) addressing diverse learning styles and abilities; these platforms require students to interact with customized resources and feedback, enhancing self-paced learning, motivation, and academic performance.
• Educational Games and Simulations: interactive digital tools designed to teach concepts through engaging, game-like experiences for the purpose of (a) enhancing learning through active participation and (b) developing problem-solving, critical thinking, and decision-making skills; they require students to apply knowledge in simulated real-world or imaginative scenarios, promoting deeper understanding, motivation, and retention of content.
• Augmented and Virtual Reality (AR/VR): immersive technologies that overlay digital content onto the real world (AR) or create entirely simulated environments (VR) for the purpose of (a) enhancing experiential learning and (b) enabling exploration of complex or abstract concepts in an engaging way; these tools require learners to interact with 3D models, environments, and simulations, fostering curiosity, spatial awareness, and deeper conceptual understanding.
• Cloud Computing: the delivery of computing services—such as storage, applications, and processing power—over the internet for the purpose of (a) enabling flexible, scalable, and on-demand access to educational resources and (b) supporting collaboration and data management without the need for physical infrastructure; it requires users to work in online environments, enhancing digital collaboration, accessibility, and technological efficiency in learning and administration.
• Data Analytics Platforms: digital systems used to collect, process, and analyze large volumes of educational data for the purpose of (a) gaining insights into student performance, learning patterns, and institutional effectiveness and (b) supporting data-driven decision-making in education; these platforms require educators and administrators to interpret visualized data and trends, enhancing strategic planning, personalized instruction, and overall academic outcomes.
• Other Technologies: a broad category encompassing various digital tools such as mobile apps, AI chatbots, IoT devices, and assistive technologies for the purpose of (a) supporting diverse educational needs and (b) enhancing accessibility, engagement, and efficiency in learning and administration; these technologies require users to interact with innovative solutions tailored to specific educational contexts, promoting inclusivity, creativity, and continuous learning.
